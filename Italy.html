Rome, Venice, & Florence Italy

If I'm being honest, Italy was one of my least favorite countries. It's just so dirty! It wasn't all bad though. I've been wanting to visit Venice and Rome since I was a kid, so how could I resist when given the opportunity. The drive from Innsbruck to Venice was THE most beautiful drive I've ever taken, and I wish I hadn't been on a non-stop bus trip, and could have stopped for photos and adventures.

Venice was a city I've wanted to visit for ages. A place of magic, in my mind. Unfortunately, it was very underwhelming. In the middle of summer, Venice was a tourist breeding ground, and I don't do well in large crouds. The city was hot, and bustling with crowds, and pretty dirty. I'm glad I went, but I don't have a desire to go back anytime soon.

Rome was next on the route. Roman architecture is awesome - and one of those bucket-list items - and Sebastian and his friend came down from Germany to explore with me. We also met up with some friends I had met previously in Dublin, Charlotte and Liz. We stayed in a pretty cool AirBnb, with a private rooftop.

My last stop in Italy was in Florence. This was one of my shortest stops, as I was only staying for the night. With that said, I went on a short city tour, went out with those in my BusAbout group, and did some laundry. I would have liked to have seen more of the Tuscan hillside landscapes, but by this point Italy was starting to wear on me. I would like to go back to Italy again to see more of the mountains and nature.
